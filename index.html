I am a fifth-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at George Mason University, working in DCXR under the guidance of Prof. Craig Yu. I received my M.S. from Ewha Womens Univeristy in 2021, where I was advised by Prof. Young J. Kim.

I focus on XR experience synthesis with Human-Agent Interaction (HAI) and spatial representation, grounded in in-context human and scene understanding. I'm passinate about how we synthesize and represent experiences in XR, specifically looking at how digital entities and humans co-exist in shared environments.

My research explores how we can design and build intelligent systems that understand human intent and context to create more seamless, intuitive, and adaptive digital worlds. I thrive at the junction of HCI and Design Computing, building the frameworks that define how we will interact with the next generation of intelligent interfaces.
